# Getting Started

Welcome to **Playlite**! This guide will help you set up your library and start organizing your games in just a few minutes.

## 1. Overview

When you open Playlite, you'll see the games you are currently playing, trending games, and recommended games based on your preferences and most-played titles.

![Playlite Home Screen](/images/app-home.png)

The sidebar is your navigation hub:

* **Library:** All of your games.
* **Wishlist:** Games you want to buy.
* **Playlist:** Your "up next" queue.
* **Settings:** Import and backup settings.

## 2. Importing Your Library (Steam)

The easiest way to get started is by importing your games from Steam.

1. Go to the **Settings** tab.
2. Find the Steam import section.
3. Fill in the required fields:

- **Steam ID (64-bit):** The numeric identifier for your profile.
- **API Key:** Your personal access key.
- Save the Steam ID and API Key by clicking **Save all Settings**.

4. Click **Start Import**.

::: tip Where do I get this data?
You can get your API Key for free on Steam's official site: [Steam Web API](https://steamcommunity.com/dev/apikey).
To find your SteamID64, you can use tools like [SteamIO](https://steamid.io).
:::

Playlite will download your games, covers, and playtime. This may take a few moments depending on the size of your library. After that, you can optionally click to fetch metadata and update game information.

## 3. Adding Games Manually

For Epic, GOG, emulators, or physical games:

1. In the **header**, click the **+ Add** button.
2. Type the game name.
3. Optional: add the platform (Epic, GOG, Emulator, Physical, etc.), playtime and status, custom cover image, and genre (action, adventure, RPG, etc.).
4. Confirm and save.

## 4. Organizing Your Progress

Now that your games are imported, click any game to view its details.

![Game Details](/images/app-details.png)

On this screen, you can review all collected information (genre, synopsis, developer, critic score, etc.) and also visit the official website or store page using the link shown.
